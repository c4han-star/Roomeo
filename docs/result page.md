# Roommate Type Survey — Developer AI Prompt / Structured Product Content Spec

## 1) Project Goal

Build a **Roommate Type Survey** experience that:

- lets users take a 12-question personality/lifestyle roommate quiz
- assigns them 1 of 6 roommate animal types
- shows a highly shareable, personality-driven result page
- allows them to share their result with a friend/roommate
- lets the second user take the quiz through that shared link
- then reveals a **compatibility result page** at the bottom of the second user’s result page

The tone should feel:

- playful
- viral
- Gen Z / Gen Alpha
- highly screenshot-able
- personality-heavy
- sharp, funny, and specific

Important:

- Keep the personality system meaningful enough for matching logic
- Keep the presentation entertaining enough that users want to share it

## 3) Survey Questions

## A. Cleanliness & Initiative

### Q1. Your roommate’s Sprite

It’s 2 a.m., you’re dying of thirst, and there’s no water left. The only cold thing in the fridge is your roommate’s unopened Sprite. They’re asleep. What are you most likely to do?

- **A.** Absolutely not. I’d rather dehydrate in peace than touch someone else’s drink without asking. **(Owl, Beaver)**
- **B.** I’m texting first. If I have to suffer while waiting, so be it. **(Owl, Turtle)**
- **C.** If we already have that kind of sharing vibe, I’m taking it and replacing it later. **(Fox, Bunny)**
- **D.** I’m drinking it now and dealing with the consequences later. **(Cat)**

### Q2. Dishes after a brutal day

You get home exhausted, make the easiest meal possible, eat, and all you want to do is collapse. Your dishes are sitting in the sink. What are you most likely to do?

- **A.** I wash them right away because I refuse to wake up and see that mess staring back at me. **(Beaver)**
- **B.** I rinse or soak them and deal with them later that night. I’m tired, not feral. **(Owl, Fox)**
- **C.** I leave them for later, but they’re still getting done today. **(Turtle)**
- **D.** Those dishes and I will reconnect when the universe allows. **(Cat)**

### Q3. The trash is clearly full

The trash is overflowing, it smells a little evil, and nobody has taken it out yet. What’s your move?

- **A.** I take it out immediately because why are we living like this. **(Beaver)**
- **B.** I’ll do it, but I’m definitely thinking this should not have gotten this far in the first place. **(Owl, Bunny)**
- **C.** I wait a bit and see if someone else handles it before I step in. **(Fox, Turtle)**
- **D.** If it’s not directly messing with me, I’m not rushing to save the house. **(Cat)**

## B. Social Energy & Guests

### Q4. Bringing someone over

You brought someone over, and your roommate gets home way earlier than expected while you’re having an intimate moment. What feels most like you?

- **A.** I’d be spiritually leaving my body. This is exactly why I’d rather give a heads-up first. **(Owl)**
- **B.** I’m texting my roommate immediately, apologizing, and trying to smooth it over. **(Beaver, Turtle)**
- **C.** It’s awkward, sure, but it’s not the end of the world if it’s a one-time thing and not causing chaos. **(Fox)**
- **D.** It’s my room. Unless my roommate has an actual issue, I’m not turning this into a whole scandal. **(Bunny, Cat)**

### Q5. Friday night roommate vibe

It’s Friday night. You and your roommate are both home, no one has work tomorrow, and the apartment vibe is up for grabs. What sounds most like your ideal?

- **A.** Separate corners, separate peace. We can coexist quietly and beautifully. **(Owl)**
- **B.** A cozy little catch-up, then everyone disappears and recharges. **(Turtle)**
- **C.** I’m down to hang if it happens naturally, but I’m not forcing a bonding moment. **(Beaver, Fox)**
- **D.** I want the place to actually feel social and alive. **(Bunny)**

### Q6. Late-night noise

It’s after 10:30 p.m. Someone’s on FaceTime, music is low, and the apartment is definitely not dead quiet. What feels most like you?

- **A.** No. Nighttime means quiet. That is the policy in my soul. **(Owl, Turtle)**
- **B.** I like it mostly quiet, but I’m not going to lose it over normal human activity. **(Beaver, Turtle)**
- **C.** Depends what’s going on and how often it happens. **(Fox)**
- **D.** I’m really not pressed about late-night noise unless it’s truly obnoxious. **(Bunny, Cat)**

## C. Structure vs Flexibility

### Q7. Bathroom war at sunrise

Every morning, your roommate is in the bathroom forever right when you need it most, and it’s starting to ruin your whole day. What do you do?

- **A.** I’m saying something immediately because this has officially gotten ridiculous. **(Beaver)**
- **B.** I bring it up nicely after it keeps happening, because clearly this is now a pattern. **(Turtle, Owl)**
- **C.** I start getting in there before them and taking my sweet time too. Since we’re clearly playing games now. **(Fox, Cat)**
- **D.** I suggest an actual bathroom rule or time limit because this house is not doing morning warfare every day. **(Owl, Beaver)**

### Q8. House rules

The sink is messy, guests keep popping up, and nobody agrees on quiet hours. What kind of system feels right to you?

- **A.** No giant rulebook. We deal with things as they come up. **(Fox, Cat)**
- **B.** We should have general rules, but people need to read the room. **(Bunny)**
- **C.** Basic house rules are good, as long as there’s flexibility when people communicate. **(Turtle)**
- **D.** Clear rules. Consistent rules. The rules stay the rules. **(Owl, Beaver)**

### Q9. Different living styles

Your roommate’s habits are pretty different from yours. What feels most like your instinct?

- **A.** I’m usually willing to adjust if it keeps the peace. **(Bunny)**
- **B.** I can adjust, but only if we actually talk clearly about expectations. **(Owl)**
- **C.** I’d rather we both meet in the middle instead of one person doing all the bending. **(Turtle, Fox)**
- **D.** I prefer one shared standard so nobody’s just freelancing the house rules. **(Beaver)**

### Q10. If your roommate had to describe living with you…

Which of these would sound the most accurate?

- **A.** “They kind of have their own system, and whether you asked for it or not, the apartment usually ends up running more their way.” **(Beaver, Owl)**
- **B.** “They’re pretty solid to live with. They’re around, they do their part, and the place feels more settled with them there.” **(Turtle)**
- **C.** “They’re not super rigid. They kind of go with the flow and figure things out as they go.” **(Fox, Bunny)**
- **D.** “They mostly stay in their own lane and don’t really get involved unless something becomes their problem.” **(Cat)**

## D. Presence & Shared-Home Engagement

### Q11. Your weekend presence

It’s Saturday. Where are you most likely at?

- **A.** Honestly, at home for most of the day. **(Turtle)**
- **B.** In and out, but home pretty regularly. **(Beaver, Owl)**
- **C.** Out a lot and popping back in between things. **(Bunny, Fox)**
- **D.** Barely home long enough to count. **(Cat)**

### Q12. Fridge disaster

You and your roommate each have your own section in the fridge. One day, you open it and notice something from your roommate’s side has leaked and is getting onto your stuff and the shared shelves. What do you do?

- **A.** I clean it up right away and tell them, because once it starts affecting the whole fridge, it’s everybody’s problem. **(Beaver, Turtle)**
- **B.** I text them and tell them to handle it, because it came from their side and they should deal with it. **(Owl, Beaver)**
- **C.** I move my stuff, contain the mess if I need to, and wait a bit to see if they take care of it. **(Fox, Turtle)**
- **D.** I’m not touching their biohazard unless it starts directly ruining my stuff. **(Cat)**

## 4) Scoring Methods

## Why 4 dimensions works well

Each dimension captures a different axis of conflict:

- mess (**cleanliness**)
- interaction (**social**)
- predictability (**structure**)
- reliability / presence (**engagement**)

Six types is more personally meaningful than “you’re a 3.2 on cleanliness,” but those numbers underneath still power matching logic.

Multiple types can share similar scores on some dimensions but differ on others, which makes the animals feel distinct rather than redundant.

Example:

- Beaver vs Turtle = both clean, but different structure/social traits

---

## 5) How the 6 Animals Sit on the 4 Dimensions

- **Beaver (Organizer):** high cleanliness, high structure, medium social, medium-high engagement
- **Bunny (Social Butterfly):** medium cleanliness, high social, low-medium structure, medium engagement
- **Owl (Quiet Professional):** medium-high cleanliness, low social, high structure, medium engagement
- **Fox (Creative Nomad):** low-medium cleanliness, medium-high social, low-medium structure, medium engagement
- **Turtle (Homebody):** medium-high cleanliness, low-medium social, medium structure, high engagement
- **Cat (Drifter):** low cleanliness, any social, low structure, low engagement

The 12 questions mapped into the 4 dimensions are enough to distinguish all 6 roommate types.

---

## 6) Coverage by Dimension

### Cleanliness & Initiative (Q1–Q3)

Clearly separates:

- **Beavers and Turtles** = higher responsibility
- **Foxes and Cats** = lower responsibility
- **Owls and Bunnies** = middle

### Social Energy & Guests (Q4–Q6, Q8)

Distinguishes:

- **Bunnies and Foxes** = more social, more guests, more noise-tolerant
- **Owls and Turtles** = quieter, fewer guests
- **Cats** = wildcard socially, but low on other dimensions

### Structure vs Flexibility (Q7, Q9, Q10)

Differentiates:

- **Beavers and Owls** = structured, clear rules
- **Foxes and many Bunnies** = flexible, situational
- **Turtles** = moderate middle

### Presence & Engagement (Q11–Q12)

Separates:

- **Turtles and Beavers** = high engagement / presence
- **Cats** = low engagement / presence
- **Fox / Bunny / Owl** = medium band

---

## 7) Final Type Logic Summary

- **Beaver:** high cleanliness/initiative + high structure + medium-high engagement
- **Bunny:** high social + flexible + medium engagement
- **Owl:** low social + high structure + medium engagement + medium-high cleanliness
- **Fox:** medium social + flexible + medium engagement + lower cleanliness
- **Turtle:** high presence + low-medium social + moderate-high cleanliness
- **Cat:** low engagement/presence + low cleanliness, regardless of social
