## 01 — The Beaver 🦫

### Hero

- Type: `BEAVER`
- Subtitle: `I'm not controlling, I'm just the only one here with a frontal lobe.`
- Signature quote: `I went ahead and made a color-coded cleaning rotation for the next six months. It's on the fridge. Please don't use magnets that aren't silver.`
- Share-card line: `The unpaid landlord — structure, receipts, and a color-coded cleaning rotation.`

### Character summary

`You aren't just a roommate — you're why the Wi-Fi still works and the hallway doesn't smell like a locker room. You have a physical reaction to a dish in the sink for more than four minutes. Your roommates are secretly terrified of your "Gentle Reminders" in the group chat, which everyone knows are actually legal threats.`

### Roomie stats

- `Cleanliness: 100% — you dream in Clorox`
- `Social energy: 60% — socializing is fine, as long as no one spills wine on the rug`
- `Predictability: 150% — we know exactly where you are and what you are cleaning at 7:14 AM`
- `Reliability: 99% — the 1% is for that one time you forgot to label the milk`

### Why you got this — fallback bullets

- `value clear expectations in shared spaces`
- `take initiative when something clearly needs doing`
- `prefer consistency in how the home runs day to day`

### Living habits

- `The Pro: You are a human spreadsheet. Utility bills down to the cent. Living with you is like a 5-star hotel, minus room service and plus judgment.`
- `The Con: A crooked rug is a personal insult. You have definitely thought about a PowerPoint on how to load the dishwasher.`
- `The Nightmare: You have definitely said, "I shouldn't have to ask you to do this," while holding a vacuum like a weapon.`

### Main-character moments

- `If someone "forgets" trash day, you have already mentally drafted three follow-ups.`
- `You relax when the kitchen passes your silent inspection — which is always.`
- `You would rather run a five-minute house sync than let resentment marinate.`
- `Guests learn quickly: coasters are not optional; they are a lifestyle.`

### The vibe check

- `clear on what "done" looks like in shared spaces`
- `confident bills and chores will not ghost them`
- `sometimes nudged toward more structure than they would pick alone`
- `secretly grateful the plants are still alive`

### Compatibility radar

- Best match — `🦉 The Owl: Soulmate — they follow your rules, stay quiet, and pay on time. Bureaucratic heaven.`
- Challenging match — `🐱 The Cat: Roommate war — chaos, mystery, unwashed bowls. Your villain arc loads here.`

### Survival manual

`Just comply. Do not argue dish soap — they researched eco-cost for four hours. Say "Wow, the baseboards look great" and watch them melt.`

### Where The Beaver thrives

- `households with simple agreements people actually follow`
- `roommates who say when plans change — early`
- `shared tidiness standards that do not require a tribunal`
- `a steady rhythm: not perfection, but consistency`

## 02 — The Cat 🐱

### Hero

- Type: `CAT`
- Subtitle: `I don't live here. I'm just a recurring guest in your life.`
- Signature quote: `Oh, was today the day we buy toilet paper? My bad, I will Venmo you in like... three to five business days.`
- Share-card line: `The mystery guest — low presence, high audacity, rent eventually.`

### Character summary

`You are the roommate equivalent of a glitch in the matrix. You exist, but no one is quite sure when or where. You can leave one dirty cereal bowl and disappear for 72 hours. You are not trying to be a nightmare; you just operate on a plane that does not include chore charts or checking the group chat.`

### Roomie stats

- `Cleanliness: 22% — the floor is just a big shelf`
- `Social energy: 50% — only when you feel like it`
- `Predictability: 5% — are you home? Another country? No one knows`
- `Reliability: 15% — you will pay rent... eventually, probably on the 5th`

### Why you got this — fallback bullets

- `value personal space and quiet time`
- `do not rely on constant interaction at home to feel settled`
- `prefer low-conflict, low-maintenance living`

### Living habits

- `The Pro: You are the least judgmental person on earth. Kitchen on fire? You say "Wild" and go back to your room.`
- `The Con: Common areas become a museum of your stuff — one sock here, a charger there, territory claimed.`
- `The Mystery: You have a roommate but you have not spoken since the Obama administration.`

### Main-character moments

- `You answer "U up?" faster than "Who took the trash out?"`
- `Your door is closed, your noise-canceling era is permanent, and your snacks are a rumor.`
- `You thrive in the overlap between "I will handle it" and "the universe will handle it."`
- `Venmo captions carry more emotional range than your house group chat.`

### The vibe check

- `low pressure — you rarely demand a performance`
- `sometimes unsure if you are home, on a plane, or a legend`
- `clear that your bandwidth is a limited edition drop`
- `half impressed, half filing a missing-person report for the sponge`

### Compatibility radar

- Best match — `🦊 The Fox: Soulmate — "dishes tomorrow" is a lifestyle. Chaos, but happy chaos.`
- Challenging match — `🦫 The Beaver: Roommate war — they have your face on a dartboard and a PDF about recycling.`

### Survival manual

`Do not wait up. If you want a Cat to do something, skip the group chat — they muted it. Corner them near the fridge and make eye contact.`

### Where The Cat thrives

- `roommates who text once, then let it breathe`
- `homes where quiet defaults are normal`
- `boundaries that do not require a TED Talk`
- `fridges with zones that are mostly suggestions`

## 03 — The Owl 🦉

### Hero

- Type: `OWL`
- Subtitle: `I'm not being rude, I'm just practicing the fine art of existing without being perceived.`
- Signature quote: `Oh, were you guys having a party in the living room? Sorry, I did not hear anything... white noise machine on level 10 and my door double-locked.`
- Share-card line: `The resident ghost — stealth kitchen runs, rent two days early, zero hallway eye contact.`

### Character summary

`You are a living shadow. Roommates sometimes forget you pay rent because they have not seen you in three days. You have mastered the Ninja Kitchen Run: cook, wash, vanish the moment a door handle moves. You do not hate people; you find small talk about weather psychologically hostile.`

### Roomie stats

- `Social energy: 5% — a 15-minute daily cap on human interaction`
- `Predictability: 95% — routine with Swiss-watch precision`
- `Cleanliness: 85% — no trace; crime-scene-cleaner energy`
- `Reliability: 100% — rent early so nobody has to talk to you about it`

### Why you got this — fallback bullets

- `adapt when plans, sleep, or work schedules shift`
- `prefer flexible house norms over rigid scripts`
- `keep energy for evenings or off-hours without apology`

### Living habits

- `The Pro: Dream roommate for peace — no stolen food, no loud parties, no hair in the shower.`
- `The Con: You text from five feet away. You have pretended to be asleep to avoid a "hey" in the living room.`
- `The Mystery: No one knows your job. Engineer? Spy? Organized vampire? Unclear.`

### Main-character moments

- `You celebrate a canceled hang more than a birthday.`
- `Your white noise could drown out a helicopter parent.`
- `You can meal-prep in stealth mode faster than anyone can say "how was your day?"`
- `If the vibe is "party," you are "notion doc titled boundaries."`

### The vibe check

- `like the apartment finally exhaled`
- `sometimes unsure if you moved out or leveled up in stealth`
- `grateful for quiet defaults and clean sinks`
- `learning that a nod in the hallway is intimacy`

### Compatibility radar

- Best match — `🐢 The Turtle: Soulmate — cozy silence, scented candles, mutual bed-rot respect.`
- Challenging match — `🐰 The Bunny: Roommate war — they want karaoke at 2 AM; you want to evaporate.`

### Survival manual

`Do not knock. Send an email, Slack, or carrier pigeon. Respect the fridge shelf — they counted the blueberries.`

### Where The Owl thrives

- `flexible quiet hours that still protect sleep`
- `roommates who text before they knock`
- `guest norms that do not feel like an ambush`
- `a home that adapts when work shifts — without guilt trips`

## 04 — The Fox 🦊

### Hero

- Type: `FOX`
- Subtitle: `I know I haven't done my dishes, but look at this vintage lamp I found on the sidewalk!`
- Signature quote: `Does anyone mind if I keep my bike in the hallway for a few days? I am thinking about stripping the paint and turning it into a planter.`
- Share-card line: `The human tornado — creative clutter, 2 AM brilliance, and a bike in the hallway "for a few days."`

### Character summary

`You do not just live in an apartment; you occupy it. Half-painted canvas in the living room, three sourdough starters, a suitcase since 2022. Fun at 2 AM; your roommates are tired of "art supplies" that are objectively trash on the dining table.`

### Roomie stats

- `Social energy: 85% — life of the party, even if the party is two people`
- `Cleanliness: 35% — not mess, creative clutter`
- `Predictability: 15% — go out for milk, come back with a tattoo`
- `Presence: 60% — hosting a salon or gone for a week`

### Why you got this — fallback bullets

- `adapt quickly when shared norms need a reset`
- `name awkward issues early instead of letting them linger`
- `prefer flexible plans that still respect roommates' basics`

### Living habits

- `The Pro: Life is never boring — best stories, hidden gems, spontaneous 1 AM tacos.`
- `The Con: Shared space is an extension of your bedroom. "Mold or science experiment?" is a daily game.`
- `The Chaos: You owe $12 from four months ago but offered good vibes or a dinner you never cooked.`

### Main-character moments

- `You fix vibes before you fix dishes — dishes are a tomorrow problem.`
- `You can de-escalate drama with one joke and one questionable DIY solution.`
- `Thrift finds migrate indoors faster than your roommates can say "lease violation."`
- `Your hobbies multiply like tabs in a browser nobody will close.`

### The vibe check

- `like they can tell you the truth without a seminar`
- `sometimes dizzy from your mode switches — social, solo, busy, chill`
- `confident small conflicts will not become a cold war`
- `aware you care about the home — just not in a spreadsheet way`

### Compatibility radar

- Best match — `🐰 The Bunny: Soulmate — vibe beats a clean floor; the apartment becomes legend.`
- Challenging match — `🦫 The Beaver: Roommate war — they have a label maker; you have a vision. Furniture may go missing.`

### Survival manual

`Lower counter expectations. Frame cleaning as an aesthetic reset. Never lend a pen — you will never see it again.`

### Where The Fox thrives

- `roommates who can talk plainly without taking everything personally`
- `guest norms that flex for real life — with accountability`
- `simple systems that can actually stick`
- `a vibe that can be social sometimes and quiet sometimes`

## 05 — The Bunny 🐰

### Hero

- Type: `BUNNY`
- Subtitle: `Living alone is for people with boring secrets. Anyway, is it okay if 8 people come over for a few drinks?`
- Signature quote: `OMG, don't be mad, but I told the guy from the elevator he could come over for a drink. He is cool, I think his name is Brad? Or Chad?`
- Share-card line: `The main character — social battery infinite, Brita filter empty, neighbors know your unit.`

### Character summary

`You do not just live in an apartment; you treat it like a content house. A roommate is a live-in audience or emergency backup for a night out. Main Character syndrome in the best and worst ways. Neighbors know your number; your roommates have a social life because of you.`

### Roomie stats

- `Social energy: 120% — battery never empty, unlike your Brita filter`
- `Cleanliness: 40% — the common area is a stage for outfits`
- `Predictability: 10% — who are you bringing home? What time? Unknown`
- `Presence: 65% — fully on or literally dead asleep`

### Why you got this — fallback bullets

- `bring warmth and social energy to shared spaces`
- `prefer friendly check-ins over cold assumptions`
- `like a home that feels connected, not just coexisting`

### Living habits

- `The Pro: You are the plug — best bars, stolen party snacks, the apartment feels like a home not a storage unit.`
- `The Con: Boundary is not in your vocabulary. 1 AM selfie consult while they are in REM is on brand.`
- `The Chaos: There is probably a random jacket on the sofa that does not belong to anyone on the lease.`

### Main-character moments

- `You remember birthdays, milestones, and "we should celebrate that" like it is oxygen.`
- `If someone is hands-off, you become the default event planner — voluntarily, loudly.`
- `You can respect quiet — you just need it communicated, not telepathically implied.`
- `Your "quick drink" has a guest list and a lighting plan.`

### The vibe check

- `welcomed without pressure to become a different personality`
- `like the home has a heartbeat — not noise 24/7, not a library either`
- `sometimes gently nudged toward more social energy than they would pick alone`
- `heard when they need quiet — especially with a heads-up`

### Compatibility radar

- Best match — `🦊 The Fox: Soulmate — disaster apartment, legendary memories, cursed Instagram Stories.`
- Challenging match — `🦉 The Owl: Roommate war — they want silence; you want a 90s karaoke documentary.`

### Survival manual

`Noise-canceling headphones + snack lock. Want a Bunny to clean? Say you are hosting — they will sparkle for the photo, not the germs.`

### Where The Bunny thrives

- `occasional shared dinners without guilt trips`
- `quiet hours that still allow normal living`
- `an "ask first" culture for big disruptions`
- `kindness as the default tone in hard conversations`

## 06 — The Turtle 🐢

### Hero

- Type: `TURTLE`
- Subtitle: `I'm not stuck at home. I'm thriving in my ecosystem. Also, is that my specialized mug you're touching?`
- Signature quote: `Are you going out? Again? In this economy? Stay here, I just started a 10-episode documentary about cults and I made tea.`
- Share-card line: `The couch CEO — packages signed, plants watered, weighted blanket PhD.`

### Character summary

`You are the main furniture of the apartment. Roommates do not check if you are home; they assume you are under a weighted blanket. A grocery trip is a major excursion. You know every creak and which neighbor fights at 3 PM. You are the heart of the home because you never leave it.`

### Roomie stats

- `Social energy: 30% — you like people, if they come to your couch`
- `Presence: 100% — always there. Watching. Judging. Nesting.`
- `Cleanliness: 80% — you clean because you have to stare at the mess all day`
- `Predictability: 100% — if there is a fire, we know you are on the corner of the sofa`

### Why you got this — fallback bullets

- `prefer steady, realistic norms over constant renegotiation`
- `notice patterns before they become crises`
- `value long-term peace over short-term "winning" the argument`

### Living habits

- `The Pro: Ultimate house sitter — packages, plants, vent sessions because you are always available.`
- `The Con: You claimed the living room. Roommates enter like a library. Pillow fluff rules exist.`
- `The Struggle: You know too much from overheard calls — a wiki of secrets you never asked for.`

### Main-character moments

- `You suggest reset Sundays, shared calendars, and "let us debrief the week" like it is therapy.`
- `Chaos roommates make you carry stress quietly until you finally speak — then it matters.`
- `You do not need constant socializing — you need reliability and follow-through.`
- `True crime counts as a social plan if someone sits on the other cushion.`

### The vibe check

- `calm when something needs a sensible fix`
- `reassured you will not explode over one-off mistakes`
- `surprised when you speak up — because it truly matters`
- `clear you are paying attention even when you are quiet`

### Compatibility radar

- Best match — `🦉 The Owl: Soulmate — outside is a scam; silent bubble and scented candles.`
- Challenging match — `🦊 The Fox: Roommate war — outside dirt and half-finished projects invade your zen zone.`

### Survival manual

`Respect the nest — remote goes back where it belongs. Party notice: 3-5 business days so they can hide. Bring a snack, earn a protector for life.`

### Where The Turtle thrives

- `early communication about schedules, guests, travel`
- `systems that do not require daily policing`
- `a culture of repair: apologize, adjust, move on`
- `consistency: good enough every week, not perfect then chaos`
